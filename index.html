Addis Ababa
Addis Ababa is the capital and largest city of Ethiopia, located at the heart of the country. It serves as the political, cultural, and economic center of Ethiopia. The city is situated in the Ethiopian highlands and has a diverse population. Addis Ababa is home to important institutions, including the African Union headquarters and the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa.
hawasa
Hawasa is a city located in the Great Rift Valley of Ethiopia and serves as the capital of the Sidama Region. It is situated on the shores of Lake Awasa, providing scenic views and recreational opportunities. Hawasa is known for its pleasant climate and serves as an important economic and cultural hub in the region.
shashemene
Shashemene is a town located in the oromia Region of Ethiopia. It is known for its diverse population, including a community of Rastafarians who settled there in the 1960s.
Adama
Adama is a city in central Ethiopia, located in the East Shewa Zone of the Oromia Region. It is situated along the main highway connecting Addis Ababa to the eastern part of the country. Adama is an industrial and commercial center, and it has seen significant development in recent years.
Ziway
Ziway, also known as Ziway Lake, is a town located on the shores of Lake Ziway in the East Shewa Zone of the Oromia Region. The town is known for its proximity to the beautiful Lake Ziway, one of the Rift Valley lakes. The lake is an essential part of the region's landscape and ecosystem, attracting both locals and tourists.
